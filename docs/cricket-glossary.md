# Cricket Terminology & Glossary

This reference glossary provides standardized definitions of core cricket terminology, tactics, and statistical metrics. Writers and editors must refer to these definitions to ensure absolute consistency across all analytical documents and publications.

---

### Powerplay
The initial overs of a limited-overs match (e.g., first 10 overs in a 50-over ODI, first 6 overs in a T20) where fielding restrictions are strictly enforced. Only a limited number of fielders (typically two) are allowed outside the 30-yard circle, encouraging aggressive batting.

### Death Overs
The final 5 to 10 overs of a limited-overs inning. Bowlers during this phase focus on defensive variations (e.g., yorkers, slower balls, bouncers) to restrict scoring, while batsmen play highly aggressive strokes to maximize the team's total score.

### Strike Rate (SR)
A critical performance metric calculated differently for batsmen and bowlers:
*   **Batting Strike Rate**: The average number of runs scored per 100 balls faced.
    $$\text{Batting SR} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Runs}}{\text{Total Balls Faced}} \right) \times 100$$
*   **Bowling Strike Rate**: The average number of balls bowled to take a single wicket.
    $$\text{Bowling SR} = \frac{\text{Total Balls Bowled}}{\text{Total Wickets Taken}}$$

### Economy Rate (Econ)
The average number of runs a bowler concedes per over.
$$\text{Economy Rate} = \frac{\text{Total Runs Conceded}}{\text{Total Overs Bowled}}$$

### Net Run Rate (NRR)
A tournament tie-breaker metric calculated by subtracting the average runs per over scored against a team from the average runs per over scored by that team throughout the event.
$$\text{NRR} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Runs Scored}}{\text{Total Overs Faced}} \right) - \left( \frac{\text{Total Runs Conceded}}{\text{Total Overs Bowled}} \right)$$

### Yorkey / Yorker
A delivery that pitches extremely close to the batsman's feet or crease line. A perfectly executed yorker is highly difficult to hit and is a primary weapon for fast bowlers in the death overs.

### Silly Point
A fielding position located extremely close to the batsman on the off-side, slightly in front of the wicket. This position is highly dangerous and requires protective gear, utilized to catch defensive deflections when facing spin bowlers.

### Googly
A deceptive delivery bowled by a right-arm leg-spin bowler. While it appears to be a standard leg-break turning away from a right-handed batsman, the googly spins in the opposite direction (turning in towards the batsman).
