# Cricket Editorial & Publishing Guidelines

These guidelines define the core editorial standards required for all cricket-related publications and content feeds within our network. Our objective is to ensure absolute factual accuracy, professional presentation, and unique value for our readers.

---

## 1. Headline Construction
*   **Accuracy First**: Headlines must strictly reflect the factual content of the article. Do not use speculative clickbait or misleading titles.
*   **Format**: Use active voice and include key entities (team or player names).
    *   *Correct*: "India Secures Semi-Final Spot After 45-Run Victory Over Australia"
    *   *Incorrect*: "You Won't Believe Who Just Qualified for the Semi-Finals!"
*   **Case Style**: Use standard Title Case for all major headlines.

## 2. Multi-Source Verification
*   **Confirmation Protocol**: All news reports—especially regarding player injuries, selection withdrawals, contract disputes, and board appointments—must be confirmed by at least two independent sources or verified directly against official press releases.
*   **Social Media**: Social media posts from players or organizations must be verified as official before being cited as sources.

## 3. Formatting Match Context
Every match review or preview must explicitly state the baseline context to orient the reader:
*   **Date & Venue**: Establish where and when the match takes place (e.g., "Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad").
*   **Tournament & Phase**: Explicitly specify the league and series status (e.g., "ICC Men's T20 World Cup, Super 8 Stage").
*   **Format**: Explicitly mention the game format (Test Match, One-Day International, or Twenty20 International).

## 4. Standard Player & Team Nomenclature
*   **Official Names**: Use standard spelling registered in official ICC player sheets. Avoid using colloquial nicknames in factual reporting.
    *   *Correct*: "Virat Kohli", "Rohit Sharma", "Kane Williamson".
*   **Consistent Abbreviations**: Standardize abbreviations for national teams (e.g., IND, AUS, ENG, SA, NZ, PAK, WI, SL, BAN, AFG) and franchise teams (e.g., MI, CSK, RCB, KKR) to prevent database key mismatches.

## 5. Duplicate Content & Plagiarism Prevention
*   **Original Analysis**: Re-writing or direct copying of match commentaries, news wires, or player quotes without substantial original analytical addition is strictly prohibited.
*   **Rewriting Protocol**: Every news summary must contain a minimum of 40% original context, tactical interpretation, or historic comparison. Always cross-link or attribute quotes to their original publisher (e.g., "speaking in a post-match interview with official broadcasters...").
