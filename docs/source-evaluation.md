# Cricket Source Evaluation & Auditing Methodology

To maintain high editorial standards, all digital resources, reference sites, and linked platforms must undergo a structured evaluation process. This auditing framework ensures that we only reference active, high-quality websites.

---

## Source Auditing Criteria

All external resources are evaluated based on five main criteria:

### 1. Language Integrity
*   **Metric**: Correct syntax, grammatical standards, and localized spelling conventions.
*   **Standards**: Primary coverage languages must be clearly separated (e.g., pure English or high-quality Hindi/Devanagari scripts). Avoid machine-translated websites with poor grammatical structure.

### 2. Topic Focus & Categorization
*   **Metric**: Compliance with our taxonomic categories.
*   **Standards**: Sites must display a clear specialization (e.g., dedicated primarily to news, tactical analysis, women's cricket, or statistics) rather than presenting a generic, unrelated list of topics.

### 3. Update Frequency & Recency
*   **Metric**: Content publishing velocity.
*   **Standards**:
    *   *High-tier (Tier 1 & 2)*: Requires daily or multi-daily editorial updates.
    *   *Reference-tier (Tier 3)*: Must update at least weekly during major cricket series.
    *   *Inactive*: Sites showing no editorial updates for more than 30 days are flagged as inactive.

### 4. Editorial Purpose & Quality
*   **Metric**: Factual consistency, unique commentaries, and original analytical value.
*   **Standards**: Platforms must avoid automated scrapers, duplicate texts, or aggressive ad layouts that hinder readability. The primary goal must be providing high-value information to the cricket community.

### 5. Content Categorization Tiers
*   **Tier 1 (Major Authority Sites)**: Highly updated, multi-author editorial teams, global coverage reach, and strict editorial boards.
*   **Tier 2 (Specialist Niche Publications)**: In-depth focus on specific areas (e.g., team analysis, domestic tournaments, predictions) with highly qualitative coverage.
*   **Tier 3 (Emerging & Reference Sites)**: Targeted fan-focused blogs, regional coverage portals, or emerging statistics portals showing consistent long-term effort.
